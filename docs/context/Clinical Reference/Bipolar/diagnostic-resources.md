# Young Mania Rating Scale (YMRS) — Diagnostic Reference

**Source:** [Young Mania Rating Scale (PDF)](https://dcf.psychiatry.ufl.edu/files/2011/05/Young-Mania-Rating-Scale-Measure-with-background.pdf)

---

## Overview

The Young Mania Rating Scale (YMRS) is one of the most frequently utilized rating scales to assess manic symptoms. The scale has 11 items and is based on the patient's subjective report of his or her clinical condition over the previous 48 hours. Additional information is based upon clinical observations made during the course of the clinical interview.

The items are selected based upon published descriptions of the core symptoms of mania. The YMRS follows the style of the Hamilton Rating Scale for Depression (HAM-D) with each item given a severity rating.

### Scoring Structure

- **Four items graded on a 0–8 scale** (double-weighted): Irritability, Speech, Thought Content, and Disruptive/Aggressive Behavior
- **Seven items graded on a 0–4 scale**: Elevated Mood, Increased Motor Activity–Energy, Sexual Interest, Sleep, Language–Thought Disorder, Appearance, and Insight

The four double-weighted items compensate for poor cooperation from severely ill patients. There are well-described anchor points for each grade of severity. The authors encourage the use of whole or half-point ratings once experience with the scale is acquired.

### Typical Baseline Scores

| Clinical State | Typical YMRS Score |
|----------------|-------------------|
| Mania          | ~12               |
| Depression     | ~3                |
| Euthymia       | ~2                |

A clinical study entry requirement of YMRS > 20 often generates a mean baseline of about 30.

### Strengths & Limitations

**Strengths:** Brevity, widely accepted use, and ease of administration.

**Limitations:** Usefulness is limited in populations with diagnoses other than mania.

### Administration

The YMRS is generally completed by a clinician or other trained rater with expertise with manic patients and takes 15–30 minutes to complete.

## Guide for Scoring Items

The purpose of each item is to rate the severity of that abnormality in the patient. When several keys are given for a particular grade of severity, the presence of only one is required to qualify for that rating. The keys provided are guides. One can ignore the keys if necessary to indicate severity, although this should be the exception rather than the rule.

Scoring between the points given (whole or half points) is possible and encouraged after experience with the scale is acquired. This is particularly useful when severity of a particular item in a patient does not follow the progression indicated by the keys.

## Item 1: Elevated Mood

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 0     | Absent |
| 1     | Mildly or possibly increased on questioning |
| 2     | Definite subjective elevation; optimistic, self-confident; cheerful; appropriate to content |
| 3     | Elevated; inappropriate to content; humorous |
| 4     | Euphoric; inappropriate laughter; singing |

## Item 2: Increased Motor Activity–Energy

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 0     | Absent |
| 1     | Subjectively increased |
| 2     | Animated; gestures increased |
| 3     | Excessive energy; hyperactive at times; restless (can be calmed) |
| 4     | Motor excitement; continuous hyperactivity (cannot be calmed) |

## Item 3: Sexual Interest

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 0     | Normal; not increased |
| 1     | Mildly or possibly increased |
| 2     | Definite subjective increase on questioning |
| 3     | Spontaneous sexual content; elaborates on sexual matters; hypersexual by self-report |
| 4     | Overt sexual acts (toward patients, staff, or interviewer) |

## Item 4: Sleep

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 0     | Reports no decrease in sleep |
| 1     | Sleeping less than normal amount by up to one hour |
| 2     | Sleeping less than normal by more than one hour |
| 3     | Reports decreased need for sleep |
| 4     | Denies need for sleep |

## Item 5: Irritability *(double-weighted: 0–8 scale)*

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 0     | Absent |
| 2     | Subjectively increased |
| 4     | Irritable at times during interview; recent episodes of anger or annoyance on ward |
| 6     | Frequently irritable during interview; short, curt throughout |
| 8     | Hostile, uncooperative; interview impossible |

## Item 6: Speech — Rate and Amount *(double-weighted: 0–8 scale)*

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 0     | No increase |
| 2     | Feels talkative |
| 4     | Increased rate or amount at times, verbose at times |
| 6     | Push; consistently increased rate and amount; difficult to interrupt |
| 8     | Pressured; uninterruptible, continuous speech |

## Item 7: Language–Thought Disorder

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 0     | Absent |
| 1     | Circumstantial; mild distractibility; quick thoughts |
| 2     | Distractible, loses goal of thought; changes topics frequently; racing thoughts |
| 3     | Flight of ideas; tangentiality; difficult to follow; rhyming, echolalia |
| 4     | Incoherent; communication impossible |

## Item 8: Content *(double-weighted: 0–8 scale)*

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 0     | Normal |
| 2     | Questionable plans, new interests |
| 4     | Special project(s); hyper-religious |
| 6     | Grandiose or paranoid ideas; ideas of reference |
| 8     | Delusions; hallucinations |

## Item 9: Disruptive–Aggressive Behavior *(double-weighted: 0–8 scale)*

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 0     | Absent, cooperative |
| 2     | Sarcastic; loud at times, guarded |
| 4     | Demanding; threats on ward |
| 6     | Threatens interviewer; shouting; interview difficult |
| 8     | Assaultive; destructive; interview impossible |

## Item 10: Appearance

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 0     | Appropriate dress and grooming |
| 1     | Minimally unkempt |
| 2     | Poorly groomed; moderately disheveled; overdressed |
| 3     | Disheveled; partly clothed; garish make-up |
| 4     | Completely unkempt; decorated; bizarre garb |

## Item 11: Insight

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 0     | Present; admits illness; agrees with need for treatment |
| 1     | Possibly ill |
| 2     | Admits behavior change, but denies illness |
| 3     | Admits possible change in behavior, but denies illness |
| 4     | Denies any behavior change |

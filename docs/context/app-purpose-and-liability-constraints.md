# App Purpose and Liability Constraints

## **Storm Tracker – Parent-Driven Mental Health Observation Tool**

### **Core Purpose**

- **Collect structured, longitudinal observations** of a child’s behaviors.
- **Map behaviors to DSM-5 criteria** only when a suspected diagnosis is **selected by the parent**.
- **Quantify and flag patterns** (possible manic, depressive, prodromal episodes) without generating diagnoses.
- **Prepare parents and clinicians** with actionable, clinically relevant data.
- **Encourage evidence-based parenting** by making observations objective and trackable.

***

### **Parent Experience / UX**

**Positive positioning:**

- Friendly, empathetic, parent-to-parent voice.
- Validates parents’ observations: “It’s not all in your head.”
- Reduces anxiety and encourages consistent logging.
- Makes patterns visible in a **non-judgmental, approachable way**.

**Protective language / framing:**

- “Track these behaviors for discussion with a clinician.”
- “Patterns may be useful to discuss with a qualified professional.”
- “Flags are generated based on DSM-5 criteria you select; interpretation is for a clinician.”

**Avoid:**

- “You have X disorder” or “Likely X.”
- “Diagnosis,” “confirmed,” or prescribing language.
- Any implication that the app decides on treatment.

***

### **Clinician Utility**

- Provides **longitudinal, structured, DSM-aligned data**.
- Summarizes **flags for possible episodes**, based solely on parent-selected suspected diagnosis.
- Exports **clinician-ready summaries**.
- All interpretation is **clinician-led**; the app organizes and quantifies only.

***

### **Data Analysis & Predictive Components**

- **Allowed:**
  - Quantifying symptom counts and trends
  - Highlighting patterns: “possible manic episode” / “signal observed”
  - Longitudinal visualizations (charts, tables)
  - Predictive alerts for preparation (e.g., upcoming challenging days)
- **Protective phrasing for predictive features:**
  - “Based on recent observations, you may want to prepare for a challenging day.”
  - “Observed trends suggest a higher-risk period; consider coping strategies.”
- **Avoid:**
  - Implied certainty or diagnosis
  - Any treatment recommendations

***

### **Symptom Context**

- Each logged symptom includes **illustrative examples** to clarify DSM criteria.
- Examples reflect **variation across gender, age, and cultural contexts**, mitigating bias and ambiguity.
- Helps parents recognize whether a broad or vague criterion **applies to their child**.

***

### **Medication & Treatment Handling**

- Medications can be logged and tracked **for adherence only**.
- Suggestions are strictly **emotional support or parenting guidance**.
- **No treatment advice or dosage recommendations**.

***

### **Disclaimers**

- Account creation acknowledgment:

> “Storm Tracker is a data collection and observation tool designed to help caregivers organize behavioral observations for discussion with qualified healthcare professionals. The analysis, classifications, and patterns identified by this tool do not constitute a medical diagnosis, clinical assessment, or professional medical advice. All diagnostic decisions should be made by a licensed clinician.”

- Footer reminder:

> “Storm Tracker is an observation tool, not a diagnostic instrument. Always consult a qualified clinician for diagnosis and treatment decisions.”

- Optional near flags:

> “These patterns are for your reference and clinician discussion only. This app does not diagnose.”

***

### **Red-Flag / Compliance Areas**

- Any wording implying the app **makes a diagnosis or treatment recommendation**.
- Automatic diagnosis suggestions without parent input.
- Confidence scores implying certainty.
- UI or copy implying authority beyond observation.

***

### **Protective / Strengthening Practices**

- Keep **parent input central**: flags depend on parent-selected suspected diagnoses.
- Use neutral terms: “possible,” “signal,” “pattern observed.”
- Anchor all tracking and predictive analysis in **DSM criteria**.
- Friendly, validating UI encourages logging while staying non-judgmental.
- Predictive features framed as **preparation support**, not clinical authority.
- Export data **for clinician interpretation**, not app interpretation.

***

### **Overall Positioning**

- A **parent-friendly, DSM-aligned, longitudinal observation tool**.
- **Empowers parents**, prepares clinicians, encourages **evidence-based parenting**.
- **Safe, compliant, and valuable**: no diagnosis, no treatment, just structured insight.
- Designed so clinicians could **recommend parents use it for a few months before appointments**.
